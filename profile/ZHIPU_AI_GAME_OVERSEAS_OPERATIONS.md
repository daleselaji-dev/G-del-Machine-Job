> This module distinguishes narrative fit from verified direct experience. The candidate has a credible game-content foundation plus overseas growth evidence, but does not yet have 3–5 years of direct overseas game-publishing experience.

## 1. Role conclusion

- **Fit type:** adjacent/high-potential transition.
- **Narrative fit:** strongest among the current Zhipu targets.
- **Direct-experience fit:** medium-low because the direct game experience is an internship and the overseas-game/community stack is not yet verified.
- **Application priority:** P1 stretch role.
- **Apply now:** yes, with a dedicated game-growth resume and a role-specific portfolio case.
- **Do not use:** generic overseas-sales resume or a pure AI-product-manager resume.

The strongest candidate narrative is:

> Game-content and growth foundation at ACE → bilingual/localized content and overseas campaigns → 0→1 international-market execution → AI-product/Agent learning.

This is more coherent than presenting the candidate as an experienced game publisher or a mature AI product manager.

## 2. JD structure

The role combines three layers:

1. **Global brand and narrative strategy** — define brand identity, global story, lifecycle marketing strategy and creative briefs for an AI-native emotional-companionship simulation product.
2. **Cold start and growth execution** — launch and grow through Reddit, X, Discord, Product Hunt, KOLs, media and data-driven iteration.
3. **Community and UGC operations** — build community culture, roles and interaction systems; work directly with seed users; turn users into ambassadors; stimulate AIGC-based UGC.

The role is not only content creation. It expects one person to connect positioning, creative judgment, channel strategy, hands-on execution, community design and growth measurement.

## 10. Role-specific portfolio — Zoooo Overseas Cold Start Case

A strong portfolio page should contain:

1. **Product hypothesis** — what emotional job the AI simulation/companionship product serves.
2. **Audience segments** — cozy/simulation players, AI-companion users, creative sandbox users and adjacent groups.
3. **Brand territory** — one positioning statement, tone of voice, visual/narrative principles and non-goals.
4. **Lifecycle GTM** — cold start, soft launch, breakout and long-term operation.
5. **Channel map** — Reddit, X, TikTok, Discord, Product Hunt, creators and media, with a distinct job for each channel.
6. **Creative brief pack** — at least five content concepts, including hooks, visual direction, English copy and why each fits the audience.
7. **Discord design** — channels, roles, progression, rituals, moderation and ambassador conversion.
8. **UGC/AIGC loop** — create → share → feature/remix → recognition/reward → return.
9. **KOL/media map** — creator archetypes, selection rules, collaboration formats and expected outcomes.
10. **Metrics** — qualified community joins, activation, D1/D7 return, contribution rate, invite/share rate, ambassador conversion, creative hit rate and user-quality indicators.
11. **Experiment backlog** — hypothesis, version A/B, metric, time window and decision rule.
12. **English samples** — launch post, Reddit discussion post, Discord announcement and creator outreach message.

This is more valuable than a generic personal website. It directly attacks the largest screening gaps.

## 14. Final decision

This role is probably the candidate’s **best narrative match**, but not automatically the easiest screen.

- It is more coherent than Harness PM because it accepts growth, content, marketing and community evidence rather than requiring a formal PM track.
- It is more personally differentiated than AMiner because ACE supplies real game-context evidence.
- It is harder than both on direct seniority, overseas game publishing, community-platform fluency and native consumer English.

The correct strategy is to **apply as a high-upside transition candidate with a targeted case**, not to claim direct senior equivalence.
