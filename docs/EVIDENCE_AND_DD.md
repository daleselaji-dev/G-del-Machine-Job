# Evidence and Due Diligence

## 1. Evidence hierarchy

Prefer, in order:

1. current official job page or verified recruiting platform page;
2. official product, investor, regulatory or legal disclosure;
3. independent industry research, customer deployment, procurement or ecosystem evidence;
4. employee/candidate platforms and interview reports;
5. public community/social signals;
6. inference from adjacent roles or business model.

A lower layer cannot silently override a higher layer. Mark every material statement as fact, inference, unknown or insufficient evidence.

## 2. Current-role gate

A role may enter P0/P1 only when the run can establish:

- current/open status or strong recent evidence;
- exact title and location;
- realistic experience level;
- application/contact path;
- no visible hard conflict with compensation/work style;
- company and product outlook pass.

A role becomes WATCH/P2 when freshness or headcount is uncertain. An expired role is not a recommendation.

## 3. Company outlook gate

A primary recommendation needs at least two independent positive evidence families, including at least one official or independent industry source:

- durable industry demand;
- product competitiveness or defensible differentiation;
- commercial/financial quality and runway;
- customer growth, usage, retention or credible deployments;
- internationalization and local delivery capability;
- strategic position in a valuable technology/value chain.

Do not treat funding, valuation, media popularity, awards, patents, logos or self-claimed leadership as sufficient.

## 4. Product-line map

For each top company distinguish:

- core revenue products;
- growth products;
- supporting products;
- experimental products;
- mature/declining products;
- products whose revenue status is unknown.

For the target role determine, in evidence order:

1. product explicitly named in the JD;
2. business unit/team name;
3. adjacent team roles;
4. regional product activity;
5. business-model inference.

Levels 4–5 must be labelled inference.

## 5. Role economics

Research or flag as unknown:

- buyer, user, budget owner and decision chain;
- lead source and self-prospecting burden;
- price/contract model;
- average cycle and PoC/sample/tender/deployment/acceptance flow;
- quota, revenue, gross margin, pipeline, usage, renewal or collection KPI;
- fixed/variable compensation and payout basis;
- ramp period and team attainment;
- implementation, after-sales, logistics, customs, inventory and collection burden;
- travel, relocation and time-zone expectations.

## 6. Employer due diligence

### Chinese companies

Prioritize 职Q company reviews, Q&A, interviews and salary modules. Cross-check with 牛客、看准、脉脉, public communities, labor/judicial information, maps and customer/product evidence.

Record:

- evidence level: target team/role, same function, same city, company, group;
- visible sample size and date range;
- recent 12-month samples;
- repeated themes;
- confidence and access limitations.

Older than 24 months is historical background. Login/index restrictions must be stated as incomplete verification. One anonymous review is a lead, not a conclusion.

### Multinationals

Separate global company evidence from China-team evidence. Verify:

- China legal/employment entity;
- China business growth/maintenance/contraction status;
- local decision rights and team size;
- whether global compensation/career systems apply locally;
- restructuring, layoffs or business-sale risk;
- English/time-zone/travel reality.

## 7. Competition map

For each top product identify:

- direct competitors;
- platform/open-source/low-price substitutes;
- non-product substitutes such as self-build, manual work, legacy system or no purchase.

Explain why customers choose this company, choose a competitor, or delay purchase. Compare capability, TCO, deployment speed, ecosystem, compliance, localization, delivery, support and channel strength.

## 8. Overseas sellability

For overseas roles verify, where relevant:

- real local customers or recurring revenue;
- certification, privacy/data and industry compliance;
- localization/language;
- local team, distributor, implementation and after-sales capacity;
- warehouse, spare parts or cloud region;
- pricing competitiveness and delivery ownership.

An English website, exhibition presence or planned expansion is not proof of sellability.

## 9. Source minimum for top companies

Aim for:

- 1 current role source;
- 1 official product source;
- 1 official financial/regulatory/business source;
- 1 independent industry/market source when available;
- 2 customer/deployment/ecosystem signals;
- 2 employee/candidate sources or a clear statement that the sample is unavailable;
- competitor evidence.

When the minimum cannot be met, lower confidence/ranking rather than filling gaps with confident prose.
